# From Manual Tester to Principal Software Engineer

In this video, James Willett shares his journey from a manual tester to a Principal Software Engineer in just three years, despite struggling with imposter syndrome.

---

## Key Stages of His Journey

### Early Career & Imposter Syndrome
After graduating with a computer science degree, James felt unconfident in writing production code and opted for a testing role, believing it would be easier. He started as a video game tester at Electronic Arts (EA), which he found repetitive and difficult.

### Transition to Test Automation
He then joined EE, where he began writing test automation and performance testing code, gaining valuable experience. He spent three years there before becoming a full-time test consultant, traveling globally to help clients with test automation and performance testing frameworks. This role gave him exposure to the full software development lifecycle.

### Breaking Point and Transition to Developer
James joined SAP as a software developer in test but found himself doing manual testing again, which frustrated him. He realized his fear of writing application code was mostly in his head and that his extensive experience in test automation had prepared him well. He applied for a software developer role at SAP and discovered that his testing background gave him a unique advantage in understanding system failures and edge cases.

---

## Strategies for Career Acceleration

James attributes his rapid rise to Principal Engineer to several key strategies.

### Visibility Strategy
He learned that even brilliant engineers often do not get promoted because their work is not visible. Despite being an introvert, he started:
- Creating internal explainer videos  
- Presenting at lunch-and-learns and internal meetups  

His manager’s advice stuck with him:  
> *“Your work is only as valuable as people’s awareness of it.”*

### Learning Acceleration
James adopted a three-part learning system:
- Dedicated structured learning time every day  
- Building side projects  
- Creating content such as courses, YouTube videos, and blog posts to reinforce and retain knowledge  

### Taking Initiative
James actively looked for leadership opportunities within his role, even small ones. He took on responsibilities like running daily stand-ups, managing sprint planning, and giving demos, which further increased his visibility. He shifted his mindset from simply doing his job to actively solving problems for his team and company.

---

## Actionable Advice

James summarizes his journey into five key pieces of advice:

1. **Don’t underestimate your current skills**  
   A non-traditional background can be a major advantage if you learn how to leverage it.

2. **Make your work visible**  
   Share what you are doing so others can understand and appreciate your contributions.

3. **Learn strategically**  
   Focus on learning new, relevant, and useful technologies. He recommends platforms like Educative.io.

4. **Take initiative before you have permission**  
   Even small responsibilities can snowball into bigger career opportunities.

5. **Create content**  
   Teaching others strengthens your own understanding and creates many long-term benefits.
